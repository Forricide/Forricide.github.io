"Please repeat that."

"The Empire is currently retaliating against the Undersiders for revealing their identities. Purity is in the midst of-"

"That's ridiculous. Why would the Undersiders unmask the Empire?"

"Well, they didn't, you did. The Empire simply-"

"I did what?"

"Sir, with all due respect, I'm not sure I understand. Your orders were to-"

"I don't understand, Storb. What could have possibly possessed me to do something like that?"

"Sir..."

Coil paced back towards his office door. He thought he had become accustomed to it by now - researching the strange histories that seemed to always exist in new continuities, discovering what his past self had done. Sometimes mistakes, sometimes strange behaviour - sometimes things that simply could not have followed from his basic thought processes.

This, however, was downright unfair. Unmasking a single cape was a crime that bordered on the absurd; to do so for an entire rival gang threatened everything that Coil stood for. Everything that he had thought he stood for, that is: the rights of free men (and women, of course) to unabashedly commit crimes without ever putting their lifestyle in jeopardy.

Coil would not stand for this.

He sat down.

"Well, it's too late to go back now. What was that you were saying about Purity?"

"She's demolishing the Docks in a search for the Undersiders, sir."

Coil consulted his past-self's documentation. In this continuity, Taylor Hebert had apparently triggered with the ability to -- summon and control flying manatees? She'd promptly joined the Undersiders, somehow being given the name Mantabee, which made a peculiar kind of non-sense. She'd - well, she'd saved the Undersiders from Lung (Manatee allergy, who would have guessed?), robbed a bank with the gang, been injured fighting Bakuda, waged war on Bakuda's gang... and, of course, attacked a PRT fundraiser.

Considering that all seemed to happen in every single continuity, this told Coil approximately nothing about her or her capabilities. But there was one obvious solution:

"Why don't the Undersiders just ride manatees out of the Docks?"

"Well, no. She appears to believe that they have a responsibility to prevent Purity from destroying the area."

"Isn't their presence in the area the entire reason Purity is doing this?"

"The manatees would get very tired, sir."

"All right, all right. I'll deal with this."

"You'll- all right, sir."

Coil dialed another number.

"Hello / Hallo, Kaiser speaking / Kaiser spricht."

"Kaiser. I'd like you to stop Purity's rampage."

"She's quit the Undersiders, sir. Moral compunctions with your mistreatment of Miss Alcott, reportedly."

But this did bring up a good reminder. Coil glanced behind him, where Dinah was practicing with a sniper rifle. "Dinah."

She lined up another shot.

"You need to wear hearing protection."

"I don't think she'll be returning to the Undersiders any time soon, sir. Seems like a clean break. Teenagers, you know?"

"Certainly, Britters."
