He leaned back in an office chair, smiling. Ten minutes ago, he'd successfully taken his revenge on human resources by commandeering one of the nicest offices using a little-known SQL injection exploit in the scheduling system. He suspected this might lead to them finally figuring it out, but it was well worth it to see the face on one of human resource's grunts as he showed them his room booking.

In order to optimize his chances of success, Coil had a (relatively) simple plan. In his first universe, he'd allow both the kidnapping and the bank robbery to progress as intended. He had already made sure that one of his henchmen had phoned in the robbery, and one of his moles in the PRT had reported by text that the Wards, sans Aegis, had been deployed. That left exactly zero superheroes in the city that were capable of responding to the kidnapping. Well, except for New Wave. Thankfully, he was confident that they couldn't beat a cornered single parent, let alone a team of trained mercenaries.

While he was coordinating with Assault in that reality, Coil would keep the robbery from starting in the other; by staggering the start time, he was sure to already know of any potential problems that might arise in the first.

This was an incredibly clever application of his power, and Coil's only regret was that he had nobody to gloat to.

In the second reality, his phone rang.
