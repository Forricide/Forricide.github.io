For years, Coil had used his unrestricted access to two worlds in a delicate manner. A reality could be a way to find some emotional release, gain information invisibly, or make high-reward plays with absolutely no risk. Over that time, his power had always necessitated a delicate balance. With exactly two realities, he had to meticulously plan each use to make sure he wouldn't have to close one reality early to get drunk.

But what could he do with sixteen?

In one universe, he drank tea with Lung, discussing the most recent edition of Supervillain Seasonals (Illegal Teens: How to recruit youth for your gang). Lung argued that the more impressionable everyday citizens were perfect targets for gang membership; Coil tried in vain to convince him that well-paid mercenaries were far better at following instructions. Lung balked at the thought of lowered profits; Coil insisted that Black Friday deals were great for mercenaries.

In another reality, he took in the sight that was his desk. One, two, four... twenty-three empty beer cans. An impressive run. He got up to use the washroom, drink still in hand.

Five more worlds were taken up with similar tasks; in some others, he sent his underlings out on moderately risky adventures.

Most of them seemed to be hilarious failures.

The less observant observer might wonder how Coil had ended up in this position. Had he been given a power-up from the heinous conspiracy that ruled the Earth from behind the scenes? Don't be silly, as if something like that would exist. Had he simply solved the riddle of powers himself, cracking their nature open to be profited from? As likely a future as that was, it hadn't happened here (yet).

In fact, Coil didn't even have the slightest idea how he'd ended up in this continuity, with all sixteen realities already created and ready to use. He hadn't done much since then, other than revel in being present in sixteen parallel universes; over the past day, he'd just barely started to get used to it. If he'd been apathetic to things before, well...

He hadn't been entirely idle over the past days. First, he was now quite certain that the activation of his power - specifically, when he split realities, not when he merged them - was the catalyst for the odd changes in continuity he'd been experiencing. Despite this confirmation, Coil still found the possibility baffling; the idea of a power being able to rewrite seemingly anything that had happened over the span of several weeks was mind-boggling.

Not that Coil's mind was particularly boggled. In fact, having sixteen times his normal cognitive capacity was rather effective in staying un-boggled. He did, however, presume that the average commoner's mind would be boggled by such a thing. Boggled to the tenth degree, as it were.

In the eighth parallel universe, Coil turned back one page in his notepad.

Well, that wasn't entirely accurate. None of this really did much to settle the unease at wondering if he himself, just like the Coil whose notes he'd been reading through, would eventually be replaced by yet another version of himself. Or was that not how this sort of thing worked - would he be pushed to another continuity instead, suddenly displaced by an unknown alternate Coil? There was no way for him to know, not unless he used his power enough times to eventually find a universe where he'd somehow acquired that information. This Coil, at least, was done trying to understand the full mechanics of what was going on. The fundamentals were more than enough.

Specifically, the fundamental truth that he was never, ever using his power again.

Sixteen realities! Sixteen beautifully crafted, perfectly separate, universes. Each connected by one great, universe-spanning being (i.e., Coil). Each providing a container for his immense genius, a vector for his majestic schemes, a tesseract of triumph.

Did it matter that he'd already essentially wasted three on incredibly petty revenge? No!
