He still wasn't quite sure how Cassandra's power worked. He knew she was a precognitive, and his notes from this continuity had small bits of elaboration on that; but, if she could see the future, why hadn't she done the prep work on capital gains tax calculations he'd assigned?

Children can be such a pain, he mused. Perhaps for his next protégé, he would choose someone more manageable - a teenager, perhaps. Surely a somewhat older protégé would be more mature.

Coil leaned back in his chair, taking the opportunity to relax this body. Even after the past several days, he was still adjusting to the need to keep his realities going as long as possible. After accidentally closing one reality, he couldn't risk doing anything that would lead to another loss.

That being said, one of his worlds had a kill order on his head and the entire Protectorate (save the heroes he'd assassinated) gunning for him. In hindsight, bombing the Protectorate Headquarters might have been going a bit too far.

But, that was fine. Sixteen - well, fifteen - worlds was plenty; he could afford to lose one, or two, or three. He wasn't making any mistakes in this one, after all.

After hanging up the phone, Coil leaned back in his chair, massaging his right hand. In one of the worlds he was in, at least. He had essentially lost track at this point, and had resorted to writing an identifier on his arm in each reality. It got him a few strange looks, but it was better than trying to show up to work at the PRT building in the reality where he'd had it demolished.

Although Coil was a rather practical, rational-minded being, he had to admit that he did find himself prone - in exceedingly rare situations - to a tad bit of stress.

His heart wasn't racing, of course (in fact, he'd been told several times he didn't even have one); no butterflies were present in his stomach (the filtration system took care of those); his thinking was hardly impaired (he'd be a genius with even half his current brainpower, so it didn't really matter) - and yet, some small part of himself was worried. Worried that this extraction would go wrong. Worried that that gang would get her hands on his new favourite underling. Worried that he wouldn't be able to get another power boost.

In one world, he stood up from a park bench. Something above the movement was reassuring; maybe it was the calming effect of not-quite-exercise - or maybe how he got a slightly better view of the smouldering detritus that was once the PRT headquarters.

Freedom?

That was nonsensical. He was already free; if losing the power boost in one reality caused a cascade to occur, he could simply reactivate his power and hunt for another usable continuity. There was no reason to feel this much stress over such ephemeral acquisitions - right? His successes in this continuity had hardly even been his in the first place; a past self he'd never been had recruited Dinah and Worker Ant, and his sixteen realities had been practically handed to him on a platter.

There was no reason to feel anxious over losing this reality. No reason to concern himself with losing access to the largest power advantage he'd ever had. No reason to worry about failing his protégé, who could cease to exist at his whim.

No. He couldn't take that risk. He couldn't lose this world, this continuity.
