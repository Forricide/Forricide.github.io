Coil steepled his fingers. When he spoke, his voice was smooth: "That should be everyone. Seems Lung won’t be coming, though I doubt any of us are surprised, given the subject of tonight’s discussion."

Suffice it to say, he had absolutely no interest in keeping this timeline. All else aside, managing two groups of teenagers would annihilate his writing time.

"Good riddance or not, Newter is right," Faultline said. "We are mercenaries. If someone pays us to deal with the Master in our midst, we may consider taking them up on it. Tattletale, sitting across from Queen Ant, groaned.

"So, I heard you had a run-in with the Undersiders recently," Thomas said, sipping at a warm cup of sake.

As Coil had skipped across continuities, each one with more accumulated changes than the last, he'd slowly discovered that he was not the most common Coil.

Well, that was not the best way to put it. It was perhaps more accurate to state that he was not a normal Coil. He was, of course, above average - but the literate reader would already know this.

Frankly, the past-Coils he'd been learning about were simply bizarre in their methods. Only a few continuities ago, he'd found notes suggesting that his past self had planned to (presumably) hire Dinah Alcott with drugs. Hire! Had his past self not understood how expensive drugs could be? Or - worse? - had they simply undervalued their own mentorship so much that they hadn't realized that Alcott would surely have jumped at such an excellent opportunity?

That wasn't the least of it. Other than the current continuity's Coil hiring the Travelers (a massive financial and mental burden, and frankly, willingly placing a monster in his own basement was idiotic, at least for someone with the brainpower and predictive capabilities of Coil) he'd counted no less than four instances of past-Coils hiring Tattletale at gunpoint. At gunpoint! Not only was that against the unwritten rules, it was frankly barbaric.

In each of those continuities, he'd made sure to take the time to let Tattletale know that he had no interest in ever having her shot. Instead, if she betrayed him, he'd simply release his Tattletale x Faultline fanfiction. He'd found that a far better motivator, thus far.

Regardless, this information had presented a secondary problem. Not only did he need to research the current state of things - as the snowball effect from a single parahuman's power being different was, frankly, absurd - he also had to account for the fact that his past-self may have done some very strange things. As improbably as it may have been, he'd even discovered that some hadn't read Being a Supervillain for Dummies.

For shame.

Fortunately, Coil had a genius idea for how to regain some of his reputation losses. After the Undersiders had released an advertisement for a re-release of Minesweeper, Bakuda's already uncommon forays into contested territory had completely stopped. The Protectorate had taken her into custody in the morning; an informer called the event "disturbing".

The Protectorate had taken the good PR in stride. Coil, as Thomas, had received several emails regarding a potential fundraiser being planned for the weekend. Coil supposed that with Lung's gang off the map, Armsmaster had finally been able to take a day off to sign underwear, or whatever he did at fundraisers.

A dozen heroes or heroically aligned, superhuman individuals would all be present at the fundraiser, with their guards let down. Perhaps drinking, even.

This was an excellent opportunity.

In fact, Coil had created a plan long ago for this very occasion.

In both realities (which the astute reader will understand had been kept in sync for this very plan), Coil phoned Tattletale.

"Oh hey, Coil. I was just going to call you."

"Ah, then you have already predicted my proposition," Coil said in both realities, leaning back in his chair.

"Uh, no, actually. I was going to ask if you'd heard of Pepper Smith's Bottled Water."

Coil grimaced. "Bottled water? That's a horrific waste of plastic." Frankly, nothing made Coil angrier than such a blatant environmental concern. You could hardly take over the world if it was covered in plastic.

"I thought you'd agree. Have you seen their current marketing campaign? They're telling people that 'When you return your empty bottles, we'll bring them back to the mountain we collected your Fresh Watertm from!"

"No! How could they?!" Coil slammed his desk with one hand, wine bottle wobbling precariously.

"I know, right? It's crazy. Anyways, what'd you have for me?"

Coil punched the wall. His hand was, unfortunately, slightly less resilient. "Frankly, Tattletale, that news is quite upsetting." He took a deep breath, not to calm himself, but to deal with the pain of bruising his knuckles. "But no matter. I will deal with that in due time. The PRT will be holding a fundraiser on Saturday. I want you to be there, and create a distraction for me."

"I'm sorry, what? Why? And a distraction for what, exactly?"

"Irrelevant. I will pay the Undersiders-" Coil briefly glanced at the financial documents he had open, then realized they were irrelevant, considering he planned to abandon this continuity within a week- "two hundred thousand dollars, total, to create this distraction."

Silence.

"I believe it is a fair sum," Coil said.

"Yeah, no, that's fair. I'll discuss it with the team."

"Goodbye, Tattletale." Coil hang up the phone.

This was his chance. Something he'd always wanted to do, but had always been prevented from doing through circumstance, or bad timing, or the fact that - frankly - the entire concept was rather inefficient.

A monologue.
